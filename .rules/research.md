# Research Rules

**Purpose:** Keep Impulse a research-driven product — coaching grounded in the strongest available evidence, novelty labeled as hypothesis, and research wired into architecture rather than filed away. **Scope:** the `research/` corpus, any behavioral claim the product acts on, and any change that cites research as justification. Implements `decisions/0005-product-never-outruns-evidence.md`, `../docs/15 Constitution.md §4` (non-negotiable #6), and `../docs/10 Engineering Principles.md §9`.

The governing creed: **Research informs the model. The model informs the product. The product never outruns the evidence.** Reality never adapts to the architecture; the architecture adapts to reality.

1. Every material scientific claim in a research document MUST carry a citation (or a clearly-marked citation placeholder) AND a source-class label + evidence tier — WHY: `research/00` §2/§7 — an unlabeled claim hides how much we should trust it, which is how speculation becomes assumption.
2. You MUST NEVER fabricate a paper, author, finding, effect size, or citation — WHY: a single invented citation poisons the trust that is the entire point of a research-driven company; describe qualitatively when you cannot verify a figure.
3. A popular author (Huberman, Clear, Duhigg) or product thinker (Jobs, Rams, Krug, Christensen) MUST be labeled `〔Pop〕`/`〔PInt〕` and NEVER presented as scientific consensus — WHY: popularity is not evidence (`research/00` §2); their value is interpretation and product principle, not primary support.
4. Sources SHOULD be prioritized in order — meta-analyses/systematic reviews → major peer-reviewed studies → authoritative academic books/established models → primary works of named thinkers → popular summaries (secondary only) — WHY: the strength of a coaching claim is the strength of its weakest supporting link.
5. Where evidence is weak, conflicting, or replication-troubled, the document MUST say so in the same breath as the claim — WHY: `research/00` §5 — false confidence is the failure mode this whole corpus exists to prevent.
6. Any behavioral claim the **Coaching Engine** acts on MUST trace to a source of a stated class, and MUST be grounded in the strongest available evidence for that claim — WHY: we coach real people at their weakest moment; "a book said so" is not grounds for a claim presented as fact.
7. A novel or proprietary Impulse model MUST be labeled a hypothesis `〔IH〕` — internally and to the user where surfaced — until validated through user research or experimentation — WHY: non-negotiable #6; we never let intuition wear the costume of science.
8. The product MUST NEVER represent speculation, practitioner heuristic, popular claim, or philosophical stance to a user as established empirical fact — WHY: `../docs/15 Constitution.md §6` red line — dressing intuition as science is a manipulation.
9. Every research document (01–10) MUST end with an **"Architecture Impact"** section answering: what changed, why (tier + source class), which architecture documents to update, and what assumptions to challenge — WHY: research is an input into architecture, not documentation (`research/00` §8).
10. A research document MUST NOT silently edit the architecture documents (`03`, `04`, `05`, `06`, `07`, `08`, `13`, `14`, `15`, `09`); it RECOMMENDS, and changes flow through the normal review + ADR/PDR gate — WHY: the reasoning must be recorded and the engine seams kept clean (`../docs/10 Engineering Principles.md §1, §9`).
11. When new evidence CONTRADICTS a live architectural assumption, the Architecture Impact section MUST name that assumption explicitly — WHY: an unstated contradiction is a silent bet against reality; we surface it so the architecture can adapt.
12. Sensitive-tier review of a coaching or prompt change MUST verify claim provenance (that asserted mechanisms trace to their cited source class) — WHY: this is where the evidence discipline is actually enforced against shipped behavior, not just prose.

**How this is enforced:** the `research/00 Method & Evidence Standard.md` standard and its per-document self-review; the mandatory Architecture Impact section (checked in research review); provenance review inside the Sensitive-tier gate (`.rules/reviews.md`, `.claude/skills/behavioral-review`, `.claude/skills/prompt-review`); and `decisions/0005`. Fabricated or misrepresented evidence is treated as a launch-blocking integrity incident, on par with a shaming-language incident.
